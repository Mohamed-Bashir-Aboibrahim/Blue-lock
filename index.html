WHAT IS BLUE LOCK?

After a crushing defeat in the 2018 World Cup, Japan's Football Union recruits the eccentric and ruthless Jinpachi Ego to revolutionize Japanese soccer. His radical solution? Blue Lock - a brutal training program designed to create the world's greatest egotistical striker.

300 of Japan's most talented young strikers are isolated and forced to compete against each other in a series of high-stakes challenges. Only one will emerge victorious to lead Japan to World Cup glory. The rest? Their soccer careers will end forever.

Follow Yoichi Isagi, a talented but unconfident striker, as he enters this cutthroat competition where individual brilliance is valued above all else. Will he develop the ego needed to survive, or will he be crushed by the competition?

SEASON 1 SUMMARY

Yoichi Isagi, a high school soccer player with excellent spatial awareness but lacking confidence, fails to lead his team to Nationals. After the match, he receives an invitation to join the mysterious Blue Lock program.

Inside Blue Lock, 300 strikers are divided into groups and forced to compete in various challenges. Isagi quickly learns that cooperation means nothing here - only goals matter. He faces off against incredible talents like:

Bachira Meguru

The eccentric dribbler with a monster inside him who becomes Isagi's first real friend in Blue Lock.

Rin Itoshi

The cold, analytical genius who dominates the competition with his perfect technique.

Ryusei Shidou

The violent, unpredictable striker with incredible physical abilities and killer instinct.

Through intense training and competition, Isagi begins to develop his own ego and style of play. The season culminates in a dramatic 5v5 match where Isagi must prove he has what it takes to advance to the next stage of Blue Lock.

SEASON 2 HIGHLIGHTS

The stakes get even higher in Season 2 as the remaining Blue Lock participants face off against Japan's U-20 national team in an explosive match that will determine the future of Japanese soccer.

Isagi and his rivals must somehow work together to defeat the established stars of Japanese soccer, while still maintaining their individual egos. New characters are introduced, including:

Sae Itoshi

Rin's older brother and a world-class midfielder who plays professionally in Spain.

Tabito Karasu

The cunning "Raven" who specializes in psychological warfare on the pitch.

Eita Otoya

A speedy winger known as the "Ninja" for his ability to disappear and reappear at will.

The U-20 match becomes a battle of ideologies - Blue Lock's ego-driven individualism versus traditional team play. With the eyes of the entire nation watching, Isagi must prove that his evolution in Blue Lock has prepared him to compete at the highest level.
